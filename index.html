Dear Delegates,

I feel privileged to welcome you to VJTI Model United Nations: VJMUN XVII. It is my pleasure to serve as your Secretary-General for the conference’s twelfth edition. VJMUN XVII will be held on the 7th and 8th of January, 2017.

VJMUN started its journey way back in 2006 and is the oldest MUN held by any engineering college in Mumbai. Throughout its many years, VJMUN has always been known for its out of the box agendas, excellent quality of debates and our trademark VJMUN hospitality. For now the twelfth year in a row VJMUN has attracted a wide array of participants from different backgrounds and places. Together they have created a lasting platform for an exchange of ideas and mutual understanding.

This year, under the guidance of an extremely capable executive Board, we will simulate challenging, thought-provoking committees. We present to you two exciting committees with daring agendas. We’ll go back in time with the Historic Security Council (HSC) to discuss the Bangladesh war of independence. The committee is convened to discuss measures to be taken for de-escalating tensions in East Pakistan and providing an immediate solution to the large-scale humanitarian crisis in Bangladesh. In our futuristic committee, the Futuristic Security Council (FSC) we’ll discuss the Balochistan war of independence. And while all this is going on, the International Press, like a silent watchdog, would meticulously report and present the proceedings of the committees.

In addition to the vast, ever-expanding knowledge on explosive global affairs, VJMUN promises to challenge you with unexpected, unprecedented events, crises and agendas; thus, providing you with an opportunity to prove your mettle as true leaders of tomorrow.

Our organizing team is working very hard to create an incredible experience for all of you, and we cannot wait to show you what VJMUN-XVII has in store, as we hope it will exceed all your expectations.I assure the delegates an enriching two-day committee session and an experience worth a lifetime.

I look forward to meeting all of you at VJMUN-XVII!

About Us



What is VJMUN?

The VJTI Model United Nations or ‘VJ MUN’ organized by the Debate and Literary Arts Society (VJTI) completed 11 years in January 2016. In 2017, while in its twelfth iteration, we aim to innovate further and make the event more enjoyable and accessible to everyone. VJMUN presents an environment where delegates can debate and discuss innovative circumstances and unique events, the likes of which are rarely seen in MUNs today. Through two days of fiery debate, delegates will hopefully come a little closer to figuring out their role in the world that surrounds them and develop as citizens of the world in a truly unique sense. VJMUN’s mission is to spread awareness of international issues and ignite the spirit of diplomacy among students through these UN simulations.

Who are we?

The VJTI Model United Nations (VJMUN) is the oldest and one of the most prestigious MUNs held by any engineering college in Mumbai. It is organized and conducted by the Debates and Literary Arts Society of VJTI, a student society whose secretariat comes from a diverse set of engineering streams and backgrounds. Each member of the secretariat shares a common interest in international relations and a motivation to make your experience a memorable one.

Why VJMUN?

Other MUNs need to make sales pitches because they need your monetary patronage. We don't. We pride ourselves on staging committees that are second-to-none in debate, and immaculate in organization. VJMUN also remains one of the few MUNs that continue to host double delegation committees, where negotiation and debate can occur in tandem. We are committed to delivering an amazing delegate experience (refreshments are also on the house, in case you were wondering!). Those who have been a part of the VJMUN experience do not need a second invitation to apply. Come, be a part of our legacy!

Rapporteur

1971 Bangladesh War of Independence


In 1947, Partition of British India gave birth to the Dominion of India and the Dominion of Pakistan. These two states later evolved to form the Republic of India and the State of Pakistan, which was composed of two exclaves- West Pakistan and East Pakistan, respectively. The Bengali-dominated East Pakistan viewed itself as being oppressed by the Punjabi-dominated West Pakistan in terms of language and culture, economic growth, military development and other administration-related matters.

Several Bengali nationalists, including Sheikh Mujibur Rahman of the Awami League, called for secession of East Pakistan from the State of Pakistan. The arrest of Rahman on 25th March 1971 proved to be the tipping point in East Pakistan’s struggle for independence as he proclaimed the formation of a new independent nation-state called Bangladesh.

The administration of General Yahya Khan launched a military crackdown on separatists in East Pakistan in 1971. This led to an armed conflict between the Pakistani military and the guerrilla resistance fighters of the Mukti Bahini, who were provided financial and military support by India. The war resulted in a colossal number of civilian casualties and severe atrocities were committed against women in East Pakistan.

This committee will be simulating an emergency meeting of the United Nations Security Council right before India actually intervened in the Bangladesh Liberation War. The committee is convened to discuss measures to be taken for de-escalating tensions in East Pakistan and providing an immediate solution to the large-scale humanitarian crisis in Bangladesh.

Balochistan War of Independence


Towards the end of the year 2018 the Balochistan liberation struggle has reached epic proportions with the Baloch Liberation Front occupying several key military facilities in the region. The all out war is consuming lives on both sides and changing geopolitical climates have caused the struggle to attain an international hue with significant Maritime ramifications.

The Security Council has been called to help deescalate tensions and evaluate further actions to be taken in order to restore lasting peace to the subcontinent.

Press Corps


An essential element of modern democracy is the press, which in today’s socio-political climate contains an immense diversity of perspectives. Worldwide, the press serves as the primary link between the United Nations and the global population, and as a part of VJMUN’s Press Corps, you will be responsible for maintaining this link.

Like in reality, it will be essential that the people remain constantly informed about the actions of the committees. As a delegation in this conference’s Press Corps, this will be your challenge. Unlike other traditional MUN committees, the Press Corps does not have a specific agenda. In the bulletins of other committees, the topic would be outlined and an introduction would be given.

With the Press Corps, however, there is no topic to outline, for the objective of the Press Corps is to report on the conference as a whole and this shall be done via VJMUN’s official newsletter, the MUNSTER. As you must be aware, this year we have planned two explosive committees, guaranteed to be action packed and brimming with activity and news ripe for picking. Are you ready for your VJMUN experience?
